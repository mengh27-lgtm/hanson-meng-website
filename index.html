I.

Elementary Manchu for Heritage Speakers

When the Qing dynasty fell in 1911, the traditional method for teaching the Manchu script fell silent with it. This series brings it back.

Elementary Manchu for Heritage Speakers is a four-volume instructional series of approximately 450 pages, accompanied by roughly 80 video lectures, written for heritage learners and the Manchu diaspora worldwide. It is the first modern English-language curriculum built on the traditional twelve syllables method, the syllabary-based approach by which Manchu was taught for centuries. Romanisation follows the Abkai system.

The series is under a signed Letter of Agreement with the National Foreign Language Resource Center (NFLRC), University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa. The complete manuscript is in peer review with three Manchu specialists; the series is forthcoming in 2027. The companion lecture series is freely available at youtube.com/@manjugisun1.

II.

Teaching & Field Research

Summer 2025 · Returning 2026
Manchu Language Instructor
Changbaishan Research Institute · Tonghua University partnership

Taught Manchu to a cohort of 16 students, including visiting doctoral students from the University of Chicago and Peking University alongside heritage learners: six hours of daily instruction across a two-week session, using the twelve syllables method; collaborated with university researchers to verify linguistic data for publication. Returning for the Summer 2026 session. Instruction takes place at Changbaishan (Golmin Šanggiyan Alin), the sacred ancestral mountain of the Manchus and a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and Global Geopark: teaching the language in the homeland where it began.

Ilan Boo Tokso, Heilongjiang Province

Conducted a week of recorded field interviews with an elderly native Manchu speaker in one of the last villages on earth where the language survives in spoken form, transcribing phonetic data that now underpins the instructional series.
